# Overview of Zeddal as a Note-Taking Assistant

## Key Features of Zeddal

### Flexible Input Methods
Zeddal allows users such as researchers, students, and professionals to input notes in a **stream-of-consciousness** style. This feature facilitates the capture of spontaneous thoughts and ideas directly as they occur, ensuring that the insights and reflections are preserved accurately.

### Data Storage Formats
The assistant offers flexibility in how the data is stored:
- **Raw Data Format**: Captures and saves notes word-for-word as spoken by the user.
- **Summarized Format**: Provides an option to refine and summarize the notes for more structured future reference.

### Integration with User's Knowledge Vault
Zeddal can integrate the notes into the user's existing knowledge management system. This ensures consistency and coherence with the notes previously stored, enhancing the overall utility and retrievability of information.

### Additional Uses
Beyond note-taking, [[Zeddal]] can also be utilized as a **journaling tool**. This application is designed to support ongoing, reflective entries that can be further developed and expanded over time.

## Conclusion

Zeddal presents a versatile solution for dynamic and reflective note-taking and journaling, accommodating various user needs through its adaptable input and storage options. Its integration capability with other knowledge management tools makes it a valuable asset for anyone looking to preserve and organize their thoughts efficiently.
