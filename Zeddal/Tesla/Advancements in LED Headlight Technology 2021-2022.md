This note outlines the advancements in LED headlight technology from 2021 to 2022 and subsequent models, focusing on the introduction and features of matrix LED headlights.

## Standard LED Headlights (2021 Models)
- **Type**: LED headlamps come standard.
- **Features**:
  - **Auto High and Low Beam**: Automatically adjusts between high and low beam.
  - **Adaptive Beam Angle and Leveling**: Basic adaptive features are likely included but not explicitly detailed.

## Matrix Adaptive LED Headlights (2022 and Onward)
- **Type**: Advanced LED headlamps with a matrix array.
- **Enhanced Features**:
  - **Individual LED Segments**: Many separate LED segments and pixels enhance lighting precision.
  - **Auto High and Low Beam**: Maintained feature from previous models.
  - **Advanced Adaptive Beam Angle and Leveling**: Guaranteed inclusion of advanced features.
  - **Pixel Level Dimming**: Specifically designed to reduce glare for oncoming traffic by dimming individual LED segments.
  - **Curving and Steering Responsive Beam Direction**: Provides enhanced directional lighting that adjusts to steering and road curves, significantly more advanced than the 2021 model.
  - **Software Upgrades**: Limited post-sale activation in the 2021 [Model S Plaid](https://www.tesla.com/models), with more extensive capabilities in 2022 models allowing hardware features to be unlocked via software updates.

## Summary
From 2021 to 2022 and beyond, LED headlight technology in vehicles has seen significant improvements, especially with the introduction of matrix LED headlights. These advancements include better beam management, adaptive features, and the ability to update and unlock features through software, providing enhanced safety and visibility for drivers.
